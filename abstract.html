Fuzzy Extraction for Finger Veins
 
Author: Simon Sommerhalder
 
Supervisor(s): Prof. Serge Vaudeney
 
Date: Summer Semester 2022
 
Abstract:

The ever-groing need for secure authentication mechanisms have given rise to an increasing interest in many different areas of human biometrics. This project focuses on the extraction of finger-vein biometric features from raw images, such that the resulting feature vectors can be used to feed into a biometric hash function. One of the most important steps along this way is the alignment of freshly taken images (probe images), with those that are already stored in the system (model images), ensuring that the hash does not depend on how the finger is placed on the scanner, but rather depends on the finger-vein pattern. When having both model and probe available, such an alignment can simply be found by trying all possible displacements and then taking the best one. The goal of this project was to find the best possible such alignment technique that only sees either model or probe, but never both at once. Our main contribu- tions were the division of the extraction pipeline in six distinct processing steps that can be individually evaluated, the design of various functions that improve the overall pipeline performance and an alignment method that, under certain assumptions, performs almost as good as if both the model and probe would be available.
